Contributor Agreement

Background

Xara has to continue to sell versions of Xara X in order to continue as a business. This is what has funded the past development and we hope will continue to fund the future development of the product including the open-source version.
 
Users can choose whether to use the free open source version or pay for the commercial versions.
  
This requires that the product is available under different licenses. One license is the open-source GPL licence that enables the product and source code to be used freely as per the terms of the GPL.
 
The other licenses are our normal commercial licenses that are more restrictive. Because our commercial versions include third party licensed products and code (e.g. plug-ins, Pantone color libraries and other proprietary code or data) the commercial license, as normal, does not allow copying and does not provide all the source code - which makes it incompatible with the GPL. Therefore in order to continue to sell these products Xara needs the right to release the product, including any modifications it has accepted, under different licenses.
 
Since only the copyright holder can license their work under different licenses this requires that Xara get an agreement that allows it to use any third-party contributions in this way. It is necessary that all contributors who want their work included in the official Xara versions sign this agreement, but it's only necessary to do this once.
  
Some other open-source projects ask for a copyright assignment in order to make this possible. This involves the copyright being transferred from the author to the organisation and results in the author losing some rights.
  
We are proposing a simpler, more fair approach where the author shares the copyright with Xara, so Xara can then use the contribution in the normal retail versions.
  
The Contributor Agreements provides some other important advantages to all users of the open-source project:
• It provides clarity and provenance for the copyrights on the project
• It reduces the likelihood of copyright disagreements or spurious legal claims
• We protect each other against software patent claims. Software patents are, in our opinion, a potential danger to all open-source projects. The GPL does not (yet) include patent grant or defence clauses. So in the document, we agree that you won't sue us for breach of any software patents you might have (relevant if you happen to be a big corporate), and we also agree with you that if any contribution you make happens to infringe any patents we hold, you can use that patent not only in XaraLX, but in any GPL project. This effectively eliminates the possibility of patent litigation between members of the XaraLX contributor community.
  
The document is clear and simple to understand and only a single page (just).
  
It's important to realise that as an author and contributor:
• This is an entirely voluntary agreement. Your are free to do anything with the source code subject to the terms of the GPL. It's only if you want to have your contributions included in the official Xara tree that we ask that this agreement be signed.
• You are not losing any rights. You still maintain ownership of your copyright and can license it as you wish. Your basic freedoms and copyright in your code are preserved.

Why does this agreement help you?

Here's how it helps you and the open-source community:

• It allows us to incorporate your contributions into our commercial version, so we have a better product to sell (without a commercial version, the open-source proposition is simply not financially viable for us). This helps you too, as we have more money to fund developers who are working on the open source version. More importantly, it means we only have a single tree to maintain, making it much easier for both you and us to work on code that benefits both versions.
• It clarifies the copyright and patent position of the code. Whilst the GPL is much respected, it's quite an old license. There are well known instances of companies who do not subscribe to the open-source ethic attempting to spread fear, uncertainty and doubt about GPL code with multiple contributors (e.g. SCO). By maintaining a "paper trail" of contributors, holding joint copyright ownership on the open-source version, and making sure (as far as possible) no contributor can later sue for patent infringement, we help make the open-source version immune from this sort of attack.
• It guarantees that we release under the GPL any modifications we make to files you contribute to. Under a more normal copyright assignment, we wouldn't need to do that. But we think it's important that we too share our contributions to these files.
• It protects you against patent claims. Whilst we despise software patents, we recognize that some day Xara might change ownership, and attitudes might change (e.g. SCO again). We want to ensure you, and the user community, are protected. 

You can obtain the agreement here: Contributor Agreement PDF

A simple (non-legal) explanation of each of the clauses:

1) Gives you an opt-out, and clarifies what we mean by contribution and GPL.
2) You agree to share the copyright.
3) Clarifies that we (and you) can't and won't seek to restrict what the other can do with the copyright.
4) Allows us (and anyone we license the code to) the right to use any patents you own that your contribution requires.
5) You agree your contribution is your original work.
6) Allows you, and anyone else using code with your contribution in, the right to use any patents owned by Xara that your contribution or the product requires.
7) Xara agrees that if we make any modifications to files containing your contributions, we'll make those files available under the GPL.
8) This agreement will apply to all contributions you might make. i.e. you only have to sign this once.
9) Necessary legal jargon to make it a contract in the UK.
10) You've kept a copy of your signed agreement.
